About

Executive Order 1-12, signed by Mayor Michael Nutter in April of 2012, established a formal open data policy for the City of Philadelphia.

Cities are data intensive entities. The direct and indirect output of many city government activities is data, and access to the data a city produces can provide valuable insights into how a city operates, how policies get made and how effective those policies are implemented.

Publishing the data produced by city agencies in an open format is a critical component of any transparency initiative, and establishing formal practices for making more of the data generated by cities available to the public is a fundamental milestone on the road to open and transparent government.

To read more about the City of Philaelphia's open data efforts, check out the City's Open Government Plan on GitHub
